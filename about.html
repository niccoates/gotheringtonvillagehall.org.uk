About the Hall

Over 120 years of community life in Gotherington

Our Story

Our History

Gotherington Village Hall traces its roots back to 1905, when a Reading Room was established to serve the local community. In an era before radio, television, or the internet, the Reading Room provided villagers with access to newspapers, books, and a place to gather, learn, and socialise.

Over the decades, as the village grew and community needs evolved, the hall was extended and improved. What began as a modest Reading Room has become a well-equipped, much-loved village facility — host to everything from celebration parties and fitness classes to community meetings and coffee mornings.

Today, more than 120 years since it was founded, the hall continues to serve the people of Gotherington and the surrounding area. It remains a charitable institution, run entirely by volunteers, and exists for the benefit of the whole community.

Key Dates

  • 1905 Reading Room founded
  • 1974 Registered charity status
  • Today 120+ years of service

Who we are

The Committee

The hall is managed by the Gotherington Village Hall Management Committee (GVHMC) — a small group of dedicated volunteers from the local community who give their time to keep the hall running for everyone.

The committee is constituted as a charitable organisation (Registered Charity No. 274869) with a board of trustees who oversee the hall's finances, maintenance, hire arrangements, and community activities.

Meetings are held every two months to review the hall's operation, discuss improvements, and plan future activities. Decisions are made collectively, with the hall's community benefit always at the forefront.

Want to get involved? The committee welcomes new members and volunteers from the local community. If you're interested in supporting the hall, please get in touch via our contact page.

Charity

Our Charitable Status

As a registered charity, the village hall exists solely for the public benefit of the local community. Hire income goes towards maintenance, running costs, and improvements to the facility. Any surplus is reinvested into the hall.

We are grateful to everyone who uses the hall, hires it for events, or supports us in other ways — your contributions directly help us maintain a valued community asset for future generations.
